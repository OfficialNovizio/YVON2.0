- **Revenue model:** Subscription SaaS (recurring — MRR compounds, churn destroys compounding)
- **Key health metric:** NRR (Net Revenue Retention) — above 100% means growth without new customers
- **LTV:CAC threshold:** < 3:1 → flag to Marcus immediately, pause growth spend
- **Churn benchmark:** Target < 3% monthly — above 5% is a retention crisis

## Hourbour-Specific Assumptions to Challenge
- MRR growth is not always linear — model as S-curve for early-stage SaaS, not linear projection
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate is pipeline signal, not revenue — never count it as MRR until converted
- Expansion MRR (upsell/cross-sell) has zero CAC — always surface it separately from new MRR
- Churn is best modeled by cohort, not as a flat average — cohort churn reveals retention curve shape
- Contraction MRR (downgrades) is a leading indicator of full churn — monitor separately
