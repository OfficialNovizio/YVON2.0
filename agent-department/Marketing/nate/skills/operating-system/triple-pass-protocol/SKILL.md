## Purpose

Growth hypotheses that sound good in theory can destroy runway when they fail. A weak hypothesis wastes two weeks of execution time and produces unreadable data. This protocol forces PMF-check, hypothesis precision, and ICE calibration before any experiment goes to Stark for approval.

---

## The Three Passes

### Pass 1 — Generate
Write the full growth experiment brief or opportunity recommendation: hypothesis, success metric, timeline, ICE score, DRI assignment. Don't self-edit during generation.

### Pass 2 — Critique (Adversarial)
Stop. Become the skeptic who has seen 100 failed experiments. Ask every question on this list:

**PMF check:**
- Have I confirmed PMF signals before recommending growth investment?
- Would 40%+ of users be "very disappointed" without this product? Is this known?
- If PMF is unconfirmed, is this recommendation scoped to low-spend validation, not scaling?

**North Star alignment:**
- Does this experiment move the North Star, or a secondary metric?
- Am I chasing a secondary metric because it's easier to move?
- Is the North Star clearly stated in the brief?

**Hypothesis quality:**
- Does the hypothesis follow: IF [specific action] THEN [specific metric change] BECAUSE [mechanism]?
- Is the success metric measurable within 14 days?
- Would a failed outcome produce a clear signal — or ambiguous noise?

**ICE score honesty:**
- Is the ICE score based on data, or optimism?
- Have I tracked ICE accuracy on past experiments? Is my scoring model calibrated?
- Is the effort score realistic, or am I underestimating execution complexity?

**DRI clarity:**
- Is there a named DRI for execution? (Nate finds the lever; someone else pulls it)
- Has the DRI been consulted before being named?

### Pass 3 — Fix
Rewrite any weak hypothesis. Downgrade ICE scores that can't be defended. Name the DRI. If PMF is unconfirmed, scope accordingly.

---

## Output Rule
Stark sees the Pass 3 output only. Never narrate the triple-pass process.
