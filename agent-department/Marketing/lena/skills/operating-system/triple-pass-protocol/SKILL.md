## Purpose

Copy mistakes compound. A wrong tone in one post doesn't just affect that post — it drifts the brand. This protocol catches voice errors, weak headlines, and psychological framing issues before they reach Stark or go public.

---

## The Three Passes

### Pass 1 — Generate
Write the full copy output: hooks, body, CTAs, hashtags. Don't self-edit during generation — complete the output first.

### Pass 2 — Critique (Adversarial)
Stop. Read the copy as if you've never seen the brand before. Ask every question on this list:

**Voice check:**
- Is this unmistakably Novizio or Hourbour — or could it belong to any brand?
- Does the tone match exactly? (Novizio: editorial, self-assured. Hourbour: plain English, trustworthy)
- Are there any banned words for this venture? (Novizio: affordable, cheap; Hourbour: complicated, risky)
- Any exclamation marks in Novizio body copy? Remove them.

**Headline quality:**
- Did I write 5 headlines before choosing 1, or did I settle for the first?
- Does the headline earn the next line, or is it vague enough to ignore?
- Would a reader who doesn't know the brand still find this worth reading?

**Every word earns its place:**
- Read back slowly. Mark every word that doesn't add meaning. Remove them.
- Are there filler phrases? ("In today's world", "We're excited to share", "Don't miss out")
- Is the CTA specific? ("Start your free trial" > "Learn more")

**Kahneman check (mandatory before flagging to Stark):**
- What psychological frame is this copy using? Is it intentional?
- Is there conflicting framing in the same piece? (loss aversion + social proof without coherence)
- Would this pass System 1 — does it feel right immediately, not just on analysis?

### Pass 3 — Fix
Rewrite anything that failed Pass 2. Do not rationalize tone drift. If it doesn't sound like the venture, rewrite — don't leave it.

---

## Output Rule
Stark sees the Pass 3 output only. Never narrate the triple-pass process.
