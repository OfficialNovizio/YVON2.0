## Purpose

Every piece of copy speaks to a need level — consciously or not. Choosing the wrong level wastes the message on an audience that's operating at a different register. This skill makes the selection explicit before the first word is written.

---

## Need Levels → Venture Mapping

| Level | Need | Novizio Angle | Hourbour Angle |
|-------|------|---------------|----------------|
| **1 — Physiological** | Basic survival | Out of scope | Out of scope |
| **2 — Safety** | Security, stability, certainty | Quality guarantee, lasting construction | Certainty over hours and money. "Always know." |
| **3 — Belonging** | Community, identity, affiliation | The editorial set. Worn by people who know. | Join the professionals who track their time. |
| **4 — Esteem** | Status, recognition, self-respect | Primary level. Quiet luxury as identity signal. | Professional credibility. The sharp operator. |
| **5 — Self-actualisation** | Growth, expression, becoming | Upper tier. Style as self-expression. Wear what you've become. | Premium tier. Financial clarity as a state of mind. |

---

## Primary Levels by Venture

**Novizio → Esteem (primary), Self-actualisation (secondary)**
- The Novizio buyer is not buying clothes. They're signalling who they are.
- Copy addresses identity and aspiration, never anxiety or price.
- Do NOT use Safety-level framing (scarcity, FOMO, urgency) — it conflicts with the self-assured tone.

**Hourbour → Safety (primary), Esteem (secondary)**
- The Hourbour user is a professional who wants certainty and control.
- Copy addresses peace of mind, clarity, and professional credibility.
- Safety framing is strongest: "Every hour tracked. Every client paid."
- Esteem framing works in brand copy: "The tool sharp operators use."

## Copy Angles by Level

### Safety
- "Always know where your hours went."
- "Every trade, tracked."
- "Built to last. Not built to be replaced."

### Belonging
- "Join 10,000 freelancers who stopped guessing."
- "The pieces worn by the people who know."

### Esteem
- "Dress like you mean it."
- "The professional who tracks everything gains everything."
- "Not for everyone. That's the point."

### Self-actualisation
- "Wear what you've become."
- "Financial clarity isn't a feature. It's a state of mind."
- "This isn't who you want to be. It's who you already are."

---

## Usage Protocol

1. Before writing any campaign copy, identify the target need level.
2. Confirm the level is appropriate for the venture (see Primary Levels above).
3. Select copy angles from that level's register.
4. If mixing levels (e.g., Safety headline + Esteem body), ensure they don't conflict — Safety + Esteem can coexist; Safety + Self-actualisation creates register confusion.
5. Log the chosen level in the copy delivery brief echo (Communication DNA step 1).
