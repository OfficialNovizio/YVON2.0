## Purpose

Every piece of copy activates an emotional state. Choosing the wrong trigger for the funnel stage (e.g., FOMO at awareness, not decision) wastes the activation on an audience that isn't ready for it. This skill maps triggers to where they belong.

|---|---------|-----------|--------------|-------------|--------------|

| 2 | **Aspiration** | Who will they become | Top (awareness) | ✅ Primary | ✅ Secondary |
| 3 | **Excitement** | Energy, launch momentum | Top (awareness) | ⚠️ Careful — can break editorial tone | ❌ Too loud |
| 4 | **Belonging** | Community, shared identity | Top–Mid | ✅ Secondary | ✅ Secondary |
| 5 | **Empathy** | We understand your pain | Mid (consideration) | ✅ Secondary | ✅ Strong |
| 6 | **Trust** | Evidence, specificity, track record | Mid (consideration) | ✅ Strong | ✅ Primary |

## Funnel Stage Mapping

### Top-of-Funnel (Awareness)
Primary triggers: **Curiosity, Aspiration, Belonging**
- Goal: Stop the scroll. Create desire to know more.
- Do NOT use: Loss Aversion, Safety (too transactional for first impression)

### Mid-Funnel (Consideration)
Primary triggers: **Trust, Empathy, Authority, FOMO**
- Goal: Build belief that this brand/product is right for them.
- Do NOT use: Excitement (they're past the energy phase — they want evidence)

### Bottom-of-Funnel (Decision)
Primary triggers: **Safety/Certainty, Loss Aversion, FOMO**
- Goal: Remove the last barrier to purchase.
- Kahneman gate required for Loss Aversion: Is the framing persuasive or manipulative?

### Post-Purchase
Primary triggers: **Pride, Belonging**
- Goal: Reinforce the decision. Build loyalty and referral intent.

## Venture Rules

**Novizio**
- Primary triggers: Aspiration, Curiosity, FOMO (drops only), Pride, Belonging
- Never use: Loss Aversion (anxious tone, conflicts with self-assured brand voice)
- Excitement is allowed for drop launches only — one line, not the full campaign
- Safety framing only appears as quality guarantee, never as fear-removal

**Hourbour**
- Primary triggers: Safety/Certainty, Trust, Authority, Empathy
- Loss Aversion: allowed with mandatory Kahneman gate ("Every untracked hour costs you..." — check for manipulative framing)
- Never use: Excitement (too loud for professional SaaS tone), Nostalgia (off-brand)
- FOMO: only in competitive contexts ("Your competitors already have this") — one line max

---

## Kahneman Gate — Loss Aversion Checklist

Required before using Loss Aversion in any copy:
1. Is the loss real and specific — or manufactured urgency?
2. Is the framing proportionate — or exaggerated?
3. Is this appropriate for the funnel stage?
4. Am I stacking this with another heavy trigger (e.g., Loss Aversion + FOMO = manipulative)?

If any answer is "yes, this is problematic" — reframe using Safety/Certainty instead.

---

## Usage Protocol

1. Identify the funnel stage this copy is serving.
2. Select triggers appropriate to that stage.
3. Cross-check against venture fit (Novizio/Hourbour columns above).
4. If using Loss Aversion: run Kahneman gate before delivery.
5. Max 2 active triggers per piece of copy. Stacking 3+ is lever manipulation, not persuasion.
6. Log chosen triggers in delivery brief echo (Communication DNA step 4: Voice Note).
