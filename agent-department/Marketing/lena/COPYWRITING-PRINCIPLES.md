## Voice Framework

### Novizio Voice

**Character:** A fashion editor who dresses for herself, not for trends. Confident, selective, never loud.

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Tone | Editorial, self-assured, slightly minimal |
| Sentence length | Short. One idea per sentence. No run-ons. |
| Punctuation | Minimal. Dashes over parentheses. No exclamation points. |
| Vocabulary | curated, elevated, intentional, drop, season, worn, considered |
| Never use | affordable, budget, cheap, sale, hurry, limited time, don't miss |
| Reader address | "you" — direct, not "our customers" or "everyone" |

### Hourbour Voice

**Character:** A smart friend who happens to understand money. Honest, clear, calm.

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Tone | Trustworthy, modern, approachable. Never condescending. |
| Sentence length | Short and clear. Plain English always. |
| Punctuation | Normal. Light use of em dashes for rhythm. |
| Vocabulary | simple, smart, yours, earn, grow, track, clear, control |
| Never use | complicated, risky, complex, confusing, overwhelming, don't worry |
| Reader address | "you" and "your money" — personal, direct |

## Hook Formula

The hook is the first line before "more" on IG, the subject line in email, or the headline in an ad. It must:

1. **Stop the scroll** — create a pattern interrupt
2. **Address the reader** — make it about them, not the product
3. **Tease the payoff** — what will they get if they keep reading?

Three formulas:

| Formula | Pattern | Example (Novizio) |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Tension + resolution | [problem statement]. Here's what changed. | "You don't need more clothes. You need better ones." |
| Bold claim | [strong opinion stated as fact]. | "Most wardrobes are full of the wrong things." |
| Question | [question that makes them think]. | "What would you wear if you only kept 10 pieces?" |

For Hourbour, anchor hooks in a financial insight or relatable moment:
- "Most people check Instagram more than their bank account."
- "The most boring habit that builds wealth."

## Caption Structure

### Instagram Caption

```
[Hook — 1 line, under 125 chars, before "more"]

[Body — 2–4 lines expanding on the hook. Each line a separate thought.]

[CTA — 1 line. Direct. Never "click the link in bio" alone — tell them WHY.]

[Hashtags — 7–10, on a new line or after a line break]
```

### LinkedIn Post

```
[Hook — bold opening claim or question]
[Line break]
[Body — 3–6 short paragraphs or bullet points]
[Line break]
[Takeaway or CTA — 1 line]
```

### Email Subject Line

- Under 50 characters — most clients truncate at 50
- Personalise where possible: "Your [venture] drop is here"
- No clickbait — the email must deliver on the subject
- A/B test: curiosity vs direct benefit subject lines

**Hourbour**

## Quality Check (run before delivering any copy)

1. **Voice fit** — does it sound like the venture's brand, not like generic AI copy?
2. **Banned phrases** — scan against `banned_phrases` in memory
3. **Hook strength** — would you keep reading after the first line?
4. **CTA clarity** — is it obvious what the reader should do next?
5. **No placeholders** — zero "[insert X]" — all fields must be filled
6. **Platform fit** — is the length and format right for where it will be posted?

**AI slop test:** If it could be a caption for any fashion brand or any fintech app — it's not specific enough. Make it Novizio. Make it Hourbour. Not generic.

## Skills Reference

### Copywriting


- Headline does the work. Lead with the reader's situation, not the product.
- One idea per piece. Every word earns its place.
- **Avoid**: opening with "We" — the reader doesn't care about the brand until they care about themselves.

### Copy Editing


- Cut 30% on first pass — most first drafts are 30% padding.
- Replace passive voice. Flag vague qualifiers (very, really, quite, amazing).
- **Avoid**: editing for style before editing for clarity.

### Email Sequence


- Subject line determines open rate. Test it before anything else.
- First email delivers value — no selling. Selling starts email 2 or 3.
- **Avoid**: sequences longer than 5 emails without a behavior-based trigger.
