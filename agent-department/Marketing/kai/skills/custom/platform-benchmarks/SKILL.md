## Purpose

A number without a benchmark is meaningless. "3% engagement" is excellent for a 500K account and weak for a 2K account. These benchmarks are calibrated to YVON's venture scale — not industry averages for brands 10× the size.

---

## Instagram — Novizio (~9K followers, fashion/editorial)

| Metric | Below average | Average | Good | Strong |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|--------|
| Engagement rate (likes+comments / followers) | < 1% | 1–2% | 2–4% | > 4% |
| Reach rate per post (reach / followers) | < 15% | 15–30% | 30–50% | > 50% |
| Story completion rate | < 60% | 60–75% | 75–85% | > 85% |
| Follower growth (WoW) | < 0.5% | 0.5–1% | 1–2% | > 2% |
| Saves per post / reach | < 0.5% | 0.5–1% | 1–3% | > 3% |

**Novizio context:** Fashion editorial accounts at this scale should target 2–4% engagement. Saves are a stronger signal than likes — saves indicate purchase intent for fashion content.

---

## YouTube — Novizio / Hourbour (early-stage, < 5K subscribers)

| Metric | Below average | Average | Good | Strong |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|--------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | < 2% | 2–4% | 4–7% | > 7% |
| Average view duration (retention) | < 30% | 30–45% | 45–60% | > 60% |
| Subscriber conversion rate (subs / views) | < 0.5% | 0.5–1% | 1–3% | > 3% |

**YT context:** First 24–48h after upload spike views and distort retention — do not benchmark from the first week. Use 7-day settled data.

## LinkedIn — Hourbour (B2B SaaS, early-stage)

| Metric | Below average | Average | Good | Strong |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|--------|
| Post impressions / follower count | < 3× | 3–8× | 8–15× | > 15× |
| Engagement rate (reactions+comments / impressions) | < 1% | 1–3% | 3–5% | > 5% |
| Follower growth (MoM) | < 1% | 1–3% | 3–6% | > 6% |
| CTR on link posts | < 0.5% | 0.5–1% | 1–2% | > 2% |

**LinkedIn context:** LinkedIn's algorithm heavily boosts early engagement (first 60 min). Impressions can be inflated by algorithmic distribution — weight comments and saves more than impressions.

## Scale Matching Rule

Before citing any competitor benchmark:
- Novizio competitors must be < 50K followers. Do not benchmark against 200K+ fashion brands.
- Hourbour competitors must be early-stage SaaS (< $500K ARR equivalent scale) or explicitly labeled "aspirational benchmark."
- Violating this rule produces misleading gap analysis. A gap that can't be closed at current scale is not actionable.

---

## Benchmark Refresh

These benchmarks should be validated quarterly via WebSearch. Platform algorithms change — benchmarks from 12+ months ago may not reflect current organic reach norms.
